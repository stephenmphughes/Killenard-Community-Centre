About Killenard

Killenard is a charming village nestled between Portarlington and Ballybrittas in County Laois. The area includes the townlands of Ballycarroll, Ballybrittas, Rathmiles, and Tierhogar, offering a mix of rural beauty and historical significance.

Historically, Killenard lies along the Sli Dala, one of Ireland's ancient roads. The area is home to the ruins of a 1600s windmill on Windmill Hill and the Georgian-style Mount Henry house built in 1820. Religious heritage includes the Catholic Church of Cill an Aird, built in 1835, and the Church of Ireland's Lea Church, established in 1829.

The infamous landlord John George Adair, known for his ownership of Glenveagh Estate, is buried locally, adding to the village's storied past.

A Thriving Community

Today, Killenard continues to thrive with a blend of historical charm and modern amenities. From its community centre to local sports clubs, the village offers a vibrant hub for residents and visitors alike.
